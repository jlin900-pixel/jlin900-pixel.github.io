Research

My work is focused towards nonparametric Bayesian density estimation and predictive Bayes. Predictive Bayes refers to the increasingly-popular Bayesian framework in which the practitioner specifies a sequence of predictive distributions instead of a likelihood-prior combination. Doing so allows the statistician to avoid subjectively assigning a prior to unobservable quantities, and instead places the subjective burden on a sequence of predictive distributions.

2025

Fast Density Regression with Weight-localized Predictive Recursion

Jonathan Lin, Surya Tokdar
Journal / Conference Name, 2025
We introduce PRx, a fast, finite-iteration algorithm for conditional density estimation. Consistency is shown, and some applications are given through simulations and applied case studies. The strength of PRx comes in the fact that it has an exceptionally efficient runtime, is provably consistent, and comes with built-in tools for hyperparameter tuning. Furthermore, it arises out of the predictive Bayes framework of Bayesian statistics, which allows the practitioner to bypass MCMC procedures in favor of a simple, recursive algorithm.
